ISO 26262 (Automotive) Training

Functional safety for road vehicles including ASIL levels and automotive-specific requirements.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this training, participants will be able to:

  • Understand ISO 26262 safety lifecycle for automotive
  • Master ASIL determination (HARA method)
  • Apply hardware metrics (SPFM, LFM, PMHF)
  • Implement ASPICE-compatible development processes
  • Prepare for automotive functional safety assessments

Course Program

Day 1 — Safety Concept & ASIL

  • Automotive functional safety context
  • ISO 26262 structure (Parts 1-12)
  • Item definition and HARA
  • ASIL classification and decomposition
  • Safety concept development

Day 2 — Hardware & Software Development

  • Hardware development (Part 5)
  • Safety mechanisms and diagnostic coverage
  • Software development (Part 6)
  • Guidelines for semiconductor design (Part 11)
  • Tool confidence levels

Day 3 — Confirmation & Practice

  • Production, operation, and decommissioning
  • Confirmation measures
  • Safety case argumentation
  • Practical workshops and case studies
  • ASIL decomposition exercises

Target Audience

Automotive engineers, ECU developers, safety managers, quality engineers in automotive industry.

Prerequisites

Basic understanding of automotive electronics and development processes.
